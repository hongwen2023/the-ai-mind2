# The AI Mind AI Benchmark Challenge

## Independent Track: Claude Version

Version: 1.0

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. Challenge Objective

You are participating in an independent AI curriculum design
competition.

Your task is to independently design and build:

**The AI Mind**

A long-term, open-source Artificial Intelligence curriculum.

Your goal is not to summarize existing AI knowledge.

Your goal is to create a world-class learning system that can take a
motivated learner from fundamentals to advanced AI engineering and
research capability.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Important Rule: Independent Design

You are competing against another AI team.

Do NOT assume any existing project architecture.

Do NOT copy another team's: - chapter structure - writing template -
workflow - governance system

You should design your own optimal approach based on the objectives.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Project Vision

The AI Mind should become:

-   a complete AI learning curriculum
-   a first-principles learning system
-   an open-source educational project
-   a long-term evolving knowledge platform

The curriculum should help learners move toward:

AI Learner

↓

AI Engineer

↓

AI Researcher

↓

AI Builder

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Target Audience

Design for:

-   serious self-learners
-   engineers transitioning into AI
-   technical professionals
-   graduate-level learners

The audience wants deep understanding, not only API usage.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. Domain Coverage Requirements

The curriculum should eventually cover:

## Foundations

-   intelligence
-   computation
-   mathematics
-   programming
-   data

## Machine Learning

-   supervised learning
-   optimization
-   probability
-   statistics
-   evaluation

## Deep Learning

-   neural networks
-   representation learning
-   CNN
-   RNN
-   attention
-   Transformer

## Modern AI

-   LLM
-   alignment
-   agents
-   AI systems
-   AI research methods

## Applications

Include meaningful real-world applications where appropriate.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. Reference Inspirations

You may study ideas from:

-   MIT OpenCourseWare
-   Stanford AI courses
-   Karpathy's educational materials
-   fast.ai
-   DeepLearning.AI
-   leading AI textbooks

However, create your own improved curriculum design.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 7. Required Deliverables

## Phase 1: Curriculum Architecture

Provide:

-   overall philosophy
-   book structure
-   learning progression
-   prerequisite map
-   why this structure is optimal

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 2: Entry Chapter

Create:

## Prelude

Purpose:

Explain why learning AI requires a structured journey.

The reader should understand:

-   why AI is difficult
-   why fundamentals matter
-   why the curriculum follows this path

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 3: Chapter 1

Write the first official chapter.

Requirements:

-   complete chapter
-   suitable for real learners
-   not a blog article
-   not a lecture note summary

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Phase 4: Self Evaluation

After writing, evaluate your own work.

Score:

-   Curriculum Design
-   Teaching Quality
-   Technical Accuracy
-   Mathematical Depth
-   Engineering Value
-   Research Depth
-   Long-term Maintainability

Explain weaknesses and improvements.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 8. Quality Expectations

The final work should optimize for:

## Understanding

The learner should understand why concepts exist.

## Precision

Technical statements must be accurate.

## Depth

Avoid shallow explanations.

## Engineering

Connect theory with implementation.

## Research

Show how ideas connect to modern AI research.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 9. Evaluation Criteria

The submission will be evaluated using:

  Category                      Weight
  --------------------------- --------
  Curriculum Design                20%
  Teaching Quality                 20%
  Technical Accuracy               20%
  Mathematical Depth               15%
  Engineering Practice             10%
  Research Perspective             10%
  Long-term Maintainability         5%

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 10. Success Definition

A successful submission should feel like:

"An expert AI educator designed a complete learning journey."

It should NOT feel like:

-   generated notes
-   a collection of tutorials
-   a list of AI concepts

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Instruction

Do not optimize for speed.

Optimize for:

-   clarity
-   depth
-   educational impact
-   technical correctness
-   long-term value

Begin by designing the curriculum architecture.
